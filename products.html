Colored Pencil, realistic "Arizona Classic"

Commissioned as a wedding gift, this colored pencil creation is a realistic representation of a 1972 C-10, built from the ground up by the groom. A representation of sentimental value is depicted by this drawing. The Arizona landscape was created from an interpreted memory and reflects Karlee's attention to not only detail, but to the meaning of the piece.

watercolor, abstract "earthy tones"

This monochrome, watercolor abstract of many views of nature combined reflects Karlee's unique ability to combine realism and abstract. Nature is Karlee's greatest inspiration, emphasizing the beauty of creation. Karlee's favorite medium is charcoal, which gives her an edge when it comes to monochrome paintings and detail in shading.

Colored Pencil "True American passtime"

Not every work of art is a painting. This was a commission done by Karlee in colored pencil, but the detail is where the beauty lies. The realistic view of the used baseballs reflects every kid's dream of playing catch with his Dad in the front yard.

Multimedia: Ink, Charcoal, colored pencil "Navigation"

An abstract created by Karlee for fun, this piece shows just how passionate she is about the art she does. The idea of creating something beautiful or creative is what drives Karlee's desire to create.

Multimedia: colored pencil, watercolor "standing out in the storm"

Simplicity shows beauty in itself. This contrasting piece reflects Karlee's desire to be different. Her faith set her apart and she shows this through her various works of art.
